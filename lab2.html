Part 1: Understanding Code and Testing the Motors (25 mins)

We are using the L293D motor shield. It supports 4 DC motors (or 2 steppers) and 2 servos.

We will use the AFMotor library to control our motors:

#include <AFMotor.h>
AF_DCMotor rightMotor(1);
AF_DCMotor leftMotor(2);

void setup() {
    rightMotor.setSpeed(255);
    leftMotor.setSpeed(255);
    rightMotor.run(FORWARD);
    leftMotor.run(FORWARD);
}
  1. Plug the motor shield into the Arduino with all pins aligned.
  2. Connect two yellow TT motors to M1 (left) and M2 (right) terminals. Red/black orientation doesn‚Äôt matter; swapping reverses direction.
  3. Upload the code. It should drive your motors forward at a constant speed. It will be slow because the Arduino is powered via USB. This will be fixed in Part 2 when using the battery.
  4. Modify the code to drive forward 3s, stop 1s, drive backward 3s, then repeat. Use the loop() function.
  5. Test your loop and verify motor behavior.
  6. Checkoff 1: Ask your instructor to verify Part 1. Demonstrate forward, stop, and backward motions.

Part 2: Assembling the Chassis

  1. Laser cut chassis (x1)
  2. Motors + mounts (x2)
  3. Arduino + motor shield (x1)
  4. Arduino mount (x1)
  5. Caster wheel + bracket (x2)
  6. M2x12 screws, M3x10 motor mounts, M3x25/M3x30 motors
  7. Attach the ball caster to its mount using screws, then secure to chassis with 3mmx8mm bolts.
  8. Add wheels. Push them on fully but carefully. Then plug in motors and battery to the shield:
    ‚Ä¢ Feed servo wires through the back hole; yellow = SIGNAL, brown = GND.
    ‚Ä¢ Feed motor wires through back hole; M1 = left motor, M2 = right motor.
    ‚Ä¢ Attach power cables carefully without swapping polarity.
  9. Attach Arduino (without shield) to mount and chassis using 3mmx16mm bolts, then add shield.
  10. Attach the small servo to front of chassis.
  11. Attach motor mounts with M3 nuts in hex cutouts.
  12. Attach motors to brackets with 3mmx25mm bolts.
  13. Attach battery pack at bottom with double-sided tape.
  14. Stick mini breadboard and ultrasound sensor to servo.
