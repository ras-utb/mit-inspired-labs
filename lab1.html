Lab 1: 3D Printing a Keychain

Sessions: Tuesday 18 Nov 12‑2 PM & Thursday 20 Nov 1‑3 PM | All sessions in Room 017 (Ground Floor)

Course Overview & Objective

In this lab you will learn to design a simple 3D object (a personalized keychain), prepare it for 3D printing, print it, and post‑process it. This project helps you get hands‑on with CAD modelling, slicing software, and hardware workflow.

Software & Hardware

Software

  • Computer with CAD software (e.g., Autodesk Fusion 360) – design your keychain
  • Slicing software (e.g., Ultimaker Cura or PrusaSlicer) – prepare print file
  • Access to your GitHub Pages repo to upload design files (optional)

Hardware

  • 3D Printer (FDM type) with PLA filament, bed & nozzle pre‑set
  • Workstation/computer for design and slicing
  • Hand tools for finishing: sandpaper, pliers, key‑ring

Steps

  • Step 1 – Design: Open Fusion 360, start a new design. Create a base shape (e.g., rectangle ~70 mm × 30 mm × 5 mm), add text or logo, and an integrated hole for a key‑ring (~4‑5 mm diameter).
  • Step 2 – Export Model: Once design is complete, export as .STL file.
  • Step 3 – Slice: Import the .STL into your slicing software. Set layer height (~0.20 mm), infill (~20 %), supports (if needed), and export G‑code.
  • Step 4 – Print: Load PLA filament, level the bed, load G‑code, and watch for first layer adhesion. Print time ~30‑90 minutes.
  • Step 5 – Post‑Process: Remove the print, trim or sand any rough edges, attach key‑ring, and check your finished keychain.
